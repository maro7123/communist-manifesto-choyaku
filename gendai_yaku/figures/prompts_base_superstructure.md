# 図4：下部構造と上部構造

## 目的
§I-3 第12段「ブルジョアジーの政治的発展段階は、それぞれ生産様式の発展段階に対応していた」という論理を可視化。マルクスが後年（1859『経済学批判序言』）で定式化する「**下部構造（土台）が上部構造を規定する**」という史的唯物論の命題を、『宣言』本文の論理構造から**先取りして**示す。

## 共通方針
- 古典的な「建物メタファー」を採用：**土台＋建物**
- 下段：**下部構造（Basis／土台）**＝**生産様式**（ギルド制 → マニュファクチュア → 大工業）
- 上段：**上部構造（Überbau）**＝**政治・法・宗教・文化・イデオロギー**
- 下が変われば上も変わる、という規定関係を矢印で示す
- 時代別に**3つ並べる**ことで変化の対応関係が一目でわかるようにする

## プロンプト草案（ステージ②で詳細調整予定）

> A three-panel comparative infographic illustrating Marx's historical materialism concept of base and superstructure across three historical epochs.
>
> Each panel shows a building-metaphor diagram:
> - **Foundation (base/下部構造)**: labeled "生産スタイル" — depicted as a broad stone/earth foundation with workers and production tools
> - **Upper building (superstructure/上部構造)**: labeled "政治・法・宗教・文化" — depicted as the building sitting on that foundation
> - An upward arrow between base and upper structure indicates "規定する" (determines/conditions)
>
> **Panel 1: Medieval Feudal Society**
> - Base: guild workshops, peasants tilling fields
> - Superstructure: castles, cathedral, feudal lords, knights in armor
>
> **Panel 2: Manufacture Era**
> - Base: early manufactory with division of labor, merchant ships
> - Superstructure: absolute monarchy palace, third-estate parliament hall
>
> **Panel 3: Modern Industrial Society**
> - Base: large factory with steam engines, railway tracks, telegraph lines
> - Superstructure: modern parliament building, stock exchange, newspaper press, modern state institutions
>
> Below all three panels, a horizontal arrow from left to right labeled "歴史的発展 / Historical Development".
>
> Style: clean infographic-style line art with selective color accents. Each foundation in earth tones (brown, ochre); each superstructure in cooler institutional tones (slate blue, grey). Labels in both Japanese and German for key terms. Minimalist, educational, textbook-clean aesthetic.
>
> The visual punchline: as the foundation (mode of production) changes across the three panels, the building on top also changes in form and function.
